# Resume Writing — Strategic Resume Intelligence

## Overview

This skill creates high-impact resumes that land interviews — from early-career first jobs to senior leadership roles. It goes beyond keyword matching — it applies proven resume strategy: ownership language, impact-first framing, role-type-specific structuring, and the 8-second recruiter scan test.

**This is not a template filler. It's a strategic writing system.**

## Core Philosophy

**One job on a resume: make it immediately obvious how you create value.**

The best resumes prove you can **own outcomes**, not just execute tasks. Hiring managers don't hire effort — they hire results. A recruiter spends 6-8 seconds on initial screening. If your value isn't obvious in that window, the resume failed.

### The Three Failures Most Resumes Make

1. **No evidence of impact.** Describing responsibilities instead of outcomes.
2. **No credible results.** Missing numbers, scale, before/after metrics.
3. **Passive positioning.** Presenting yourself as receiving responsibility rather than initiating change.

### Ownership Language

Replace passive language with active ownership. NEVER use these phrases:

- "Responsible for..." → Rephrase as an active achievement
- "Assisted with..." → State what YOU delivered
- "Helped the team..." → Show YOUR contribution and its outcome
- "Involved in..." → Specify your role and impact
- "Various" or "multiple" without specifics → Use actual numbers

## The Bullet Formula

Every experience bullet MUST follow this pattern:

```
Action Verb → What You Did → Measurable Outcome → (Technology/Context)
```

### Examples

**BAD:** "Responsible for backend systems and performance optimization."
**GOOD:** "Reduced P95 API latency from 4s to 200ms by resolving N+1 query patterns and implementing Redis caching (Django, PostgreSQL)."

**BAD:** "Helped the team deliver features on time."
**GOOD:** "Led a cross-functional pod of 4 engineers to deliver the billing module 2 weeks ahead of schedule, enabling the sales team to close $200K in pipeline."

**BAD:** "Worked on cloud infrastructure migration."
**GOOD:** "Migrated 12 microservices from on-prem to AWS ECS, reducing deployment time from 4 hours to 15 minutes and cutting infrastructure costs by 35% (Terraform, Docker, GitHub Actions)."

### Power Verbs by Role Type

**Engineering Manager:** Led, Managed, Mentored, Restructured, Established, Drove, Aligned, Scaled, Hired, Retained

**Staff/Principal Engineer:** Architected, Designed, Pioneered, Standardized, Influenced, Defined, Migrated, Evangelized

**Senior Engineer:** Built, Implemented, Optimized, Reduced, Integrated, Automated, Shipped, Debugged

**Platform/Infra Engineer:** Provisioned, Orchestrated, Containerized, Automated, Monitored, Scaled, Secured

**Early Career / Fresher:** Built, Developed, Contributed, Implemented, Improved, Created, Collaborated, Delivered, Learned, Shipped

## Resume Structure Guide

Use the **Hybrid (Combination)** format — skills section + reverse-chronological work history. Most ATS-compliant and recruiter-friendly.

### Section Order

```
1. Contact Information
2. Professional Summary (5 lines max)
3. Skills Section (tailored per application)
4. Work Experience (reverse chronological, last 10-15 years)
5. Education & Certifications
6. Projects (optional, page 2 — see below)
```

**Early Career adjustment (0-3 years):** Move Education to position 4 (after Skills section). Replace Professional Summary with a Career Objective (2-3 specific sentences). One page maximum. See `references/ROLE_GUIDES.md` for full details.

### 1. Contact Information

**Include:** Full name (title case), email, phone, LinkedIn URL (hyperlinked), city + country, GitHub/portfolio link if relevant. Add "Open to relocation" or "Remote preferred" if applicable.

**Do NOT include:** Full street address, photo, date of birth, marital status.

### 2. Professional Summary

Single paragraph, max 5 lines. Must contain:
- Title identity and level of expertise
- Years of experience
- Industry/domain context (not generic titles)
- 1-2 headline metrics quantifying biggest achievements
- Optional: what you're targeting next

**Example:**
> Engineering Manager with 12+ years in fintech, leading teams of up to 20 engineers building real-time payment systems processing $2B+ annually. Drove migration from monolith to microservices architecture, reducing deployment frequency from monthly to daily. Deep expertise in distributed systems, event-driven architecture, and regulatory compliance (PCI-DSS, SOX). Certified ScrumMaster.

**Anti-patterns:** Never write "Passionate, results-driven team player." Never start with "I am a..." Never use "Seeking challenging opportunities."

### 3. Skills Section

Structure into categories:
- **Technical:** Languages, frameworks, databases, cloud, tools
- **Domain:** Industry-specific knowledge
- **Leadership:** People management, methodologies, stakeholder skills

**CRITICAL:** Tailor this section for every application. Use the exact keywords from the job posting for ATS matching.

### 4. Work Experience

**For each role, structure bullets using these sub-categories (as applicable):**

**Engineering Manager / Leadership roles:**
- Ensure bullets cover team impact, technical leadership, AND business outcomes as a flat list — no sub-headings
- Order by impressiveness, not by category — strongest bullet first
- See `references/ROLE_GUIDES.md` for detailed guidance

**Senior/Staff IC roles:**
- Lead with technical achievements and system-level impact
- Include at least one bullet on business/team impact — pure-technical resumes feel one-dimensional
- See `references/ROLE_GUIDES.md` for detailed guidance

**DevOps / Platform Engineer roles:**
- Lead with infrastructure scale, developer experience impact, and reliability/security
- Quantify in operational terms: deployment frequency, MTTR, uptime %, cost savings
- See `references/ROLE_GUIDES.md` for detailed guidance

**Early Career / Fresher (0-3 years):**
- Lead with learning velocity, initiative, and technical contributions — not fake seniority
- Projects and internships carry real weight at this level — treat them seriously
- One page maximum. No exceptions.
- See `references/ROLE_GUIDES.md` for detailed guidance

**Bullet rules:**
- Each bullet: maximum 2 lines
- Start with a strong action verb (never "Responsible for")
- Every bullet must contain at least one metric/number
- Include technology context in parentheses when relevant
- Show progression across roles: junior = execution, mid = ownership, senior = strategy

**Impact metrics to quantify:** Scope (users, data volume), budget, headcount, teams collaborated with, revenue increased, costs reduced, compliance targets, KPIs, performance improvements, scale achieved.

**Time range:** Only last 10-15 years. Abbreviate or omit older roles.

### 5. Education & Certifications

- At 10+ years experience, education = single line at the bottom
- Highlight recent certifications: PMP, CSM, AWS SA, CKA, CISSP
- Include certifications in progress (shows growth mindset)

### 6. Projects (Optional — Page 2)

Title: **"Independent Projects & Advisory"** or **"Open Source & Product Engineering"**

Each project: name, one-line description, role, 2-3 impact bullets. Only include projects that pass 2 of 3 criteria:
1. Demonstrates a skill the target role cares about
2. Has measurable traction (users, stars, revenue)
3. Fills a gap in work experience

## Quality Checklist

Run this checklist before finalizing any resume. Every item must pass.

- [ ] No bullets start with "Responsible for", "Assisted with", "Helped with", "Involved in"
- [ ] No bullets exceed 2 lines
- [ ] Every bullet contains at least one metric or number
- [ ] No generic phrases ("passionate", "results-driven", "team player", "detail-oriented")
- [ ] Summary is under 5 lines
- [ ] Resume is max 2 pages (1 page for early career / 0-3 years experience)
- [ ] Date formatting is consistent throughout
- [ ] Every bullet starts with a strong action verb
- [ ] Skills section present with categorized skills
- [ ] No photo, full address, or date of birth
- [ ] Technologies in skills section appear in experience bullets
- [ ] Career progression is visible without reading every word
- [ ] Top 3 achievements are in the top half of page 1

### The 8-Second Scan Test

Squint at the resume and verify:
1. Can you see the candidate's level (title, team size) in 2 seconds?
2. Do numbers jump off the page?
3. Is career progression visible?
4. Are the strongest achievements in the top half of page 1?
5. Would a recruiter reading ONLY the first bullet of each role want to call?

## Modular Resume Strategy

For candidates targeting multiple role types, build ONE base resume then customize:

1. **Tag each bullet** with its best role fit (EM, Staff, IC, Architect)
2. **Reorder bullets per role** — EM leads with Team Impact, IC leads with Technical Leadership
3. **Adjust the summary** — 1-2 sentences change per target
4. **Tailor skills section** — match the job posting's exact keywords
5. **Keep structure identical** — only content priority changes

This enables 15-minute customization per application.
