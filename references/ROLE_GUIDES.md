# Role-Specific Resume Guides

## Detailed guidance for structuring resumes by role type and experience level.

---

## Engineering Manager / Leadership Roles

For each role, ensure bullets naturally cover all three impact areas as a flat bullet list. Do NOT use sub-headings like "Team Impact" or "Technical Leadership" within a role.

**Coverage targets:**
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **team impact** (growth, retention, mentorship, org design, hiring)
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **technical leadership** (architecture decisions, system improvements, tech strategy)
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **business outcomes** (revenue impact, cost savings, KPIs met, compliance achieved)

**Ordering:** By impressiveness, not by category. Your single most impressive bullet goes first regardless of whether it's a team, technical, or business achievement.

**What recruiters look for in EM resumes:**
- Domain expertise and familiarity with the industry or stack
- People management evidence: team building, conflict resolution, mentorship, retention
- Strategic thinking: aligning team goals with company objectives
- Technical acumen: ability to guide decisions even if not hands-on daily

**Example bullets:**
- "Restructured engineering from siloed teams into cross-functional pods of 3-4 engineers, reducing handoff delays and developing leadership skills through rotating pod leads."
- "Reduced P95 API latency from 20s to under 2s by identifying N+1 query patterns and leading a systematic performance optimization initiative across 3 services."
- "Maintained 75-90% sprint completion rate, establishing predictable delivery cadence that enabled reliable release planning with product and clinical stakeholders."

## Senior / Staff / Principal Engineer Roles

Lead with technical achievements that show system-level impact and cross-team influence. Staff-level resumes must demonstrate you operate beyond your immediate team.

**Coverage targets:**
- At least 2-3 bullets showing **technical depth** (architecture, system design, performance, scale)
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **cross-team influence** (design reviews, standards, mentoring, tech strategy)
- At least 1 bullet showing **business/product impact** — connect technical work to outcomes stakeholders care about

**Ordering:** Technical depth first, then influence, then business impact.

**What differentiates Staff from Senior on a resume:**
- Senior: "I built X and it performed well"
- Staff: "I identified that we needed X, designed the approach, influenced 3 teams to adopt it, and it improved Y metric org-wide"

**Example bullets:**
- "Architected the event-driven order processing pipeline handling 50K+ events/second, replacing a synchronous system that had become the primary scaling bottleneck (Kafka, Go, PostgreSQL)."
- "Defined and evangelized API design standards adopted across 4 backend teams, reducing integration bugs by 40% and cutting cross-team onboarding time from 2 weeks to 3 days."
- "Mentored 3 engineers to Senior promotion within 18 months through weekly architecture pairing sessions and structured growth plans."

---

## DevOps / Platform Engineer Roles

Lead with infrastructure and reliability impact. Platform engineers are measured by how much they accelerate other engineers and how reliable the systems are.

**Coverage targets:**
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **infrastructure scale** (clusters managed, services deployed, environments provisioned, cost optimization)
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **developer experience impact** (CI/CD improvements, deployment time reduction, internal tooling that unblocked other teams)
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **reliability and security** (uptime SLAs, incident response time improvements, compliance, observability)

**Ordering:** By impressiveness. If your biggest win was cutting deployment time from 4 hours to 15 minutes, that goes first.

**Quantify in operational terms:** deployment frequency, MTTR, uptime percentage, cost savings, environments managed, pipeline execution time, containers orchestrated, incidents prevented.

**Example bullets:**
- "Migrated 12 production services to Kubernetes, reducing deployment time from 4 hours to 15 minutes and enabling daily releases across 3 teams (EKS, Terraform, ArgoCD)."
- "Designed and built the CI/CD pipeline serving 40+ engineers, achieving 99.8% pipeline success rate and cutting build-to-deploy time from 45 minutes to 8 minutes (GitHub Actions, Docker)."
- "Implemented Prometheus + Grafana observability stack across all production services, reducing mean-time-to-detection from 30 minutes to under 2 minutes and enabling proactive alerting."

## Early Career / Fresher (0-3 Years Experience)

Your resume plays by different rules. Don't try to mimic a senior resume — reviewers spot fake seniority instantly. Own your level honestly and lead with what makes junior candidates compelling: learning speed, initiative, and genuine technical contribution.

### Structural Changes
- **One page maximum.** No exceptions at this level.
- **Education moves UP** to position 3 (after Contact Info and Career Objective, before Skills). Include GPA if strong (>3.5), relevant coursework, capstone projects, and academic achievements.
- **Replace "Professional Summary" with "Career Objective"** — 2-3 sentences stating your target role, strongest technical skills, and what you bring. Be specific, not generic.
- **Projects and internships carry real weight.** A strong personal project with users or GitHub stars can outweigh a bland internship. Treat them as seriously as work experience.

### Section Order (Early Career)
```
1. Contact Information
2. Career Objective (2-3 sentences, specific)
3. Education (degree, GPA if strong, relevant coursework, capstone)
4. Skills Section
5. Work Experience / Internships
6. Projects
```

### Bullet Coverage
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **technical contribution** (features shipped, bugs fixed, tests written — with specifics)
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **collaboration and growth** (worked with senior engineers on X, ramped up on Y in Z weeks, took ownership of a module)
- At least 1-2 bullets showing **measurable output** (test coverage improved by X%, reduced build time by Y minutes, shipped Z features in first quarter)

### Metrics at This Level
Metrics may be smaller and that's okay. All of these are valid early-career metrics:
- "Improved test coverage from 40% to 70%"
- "Shipped 3 features in first quarter, ahead of onboarding expectations"
- "Reduced CI build time by 5 minutes by optimizing test parallelization"
- "Built an internal dashboard used daily by 8 team members"

The principle is the same as senior resumes: show impact, not just activity.

### What NOT to Do as a Fresher
- Don't list "Leadership" skills you don't have evidence for
- Don't pad with generic hobbies ("Reading, Traveling") — if space is tight, cut them
- Don't include every technology you've ever touched — list what you can genuinely discuss in an interview
- Don't use a 2-page resume — it signals you can't prioritize

### Example Career Objective
> "Recent Computer Science graduate with hands-on full-stack experience from 2 internships and 3 shipped side projects. Proficient in Python, React, and PostgreSQL. Built an open-source CLI tool with 200+ GitHub stars. Seeking a backend engineering role where I can contribute to production systems while growing under experienced engineers."
