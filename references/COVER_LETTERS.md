# Cover Letter Writing Guide

## Structure, strategy, and examples for high-impact cover letters.

---

## Core Principle

A cover letter is NOT a prose version of your resume. It's a **narrative argument** for why you're the right person for this specific role at this specific company. It tells a story your resume can't.

---

## Structure (One Page, 4-5 Paragraphs)

### Paragraph 1: The Hook (3-4 sentences)

Open with your differentiation — why your background makes you uniquely suited. NOT "I am writing to apply for..." (wastes the most valuable real estate on the page).

**Pattern:** State the role → your differentiator → your headline metric → why it matters for them.

**Example:**
> As a backend architect who has spent 8 years building HIPAA-compliant clinical platforms serving 10,000+ patients, I bring a perspective most candidates for the Innovation Specialist role at [Company] won't have — I've actually built the kind of digital health technology you're looking to evaluate and scale.

### Paragraph 2-3: Evidence (4-5 sentences each)

Map your top 2-3 experiences to the JD's top 2-3 requirements. Each paragraph should:
1. Name the requirement (implicitly or explicitly)
2. Describe YOUR specific experience addressing it
3. Include a metric or outcome
4. Connect it to what the company needs

**Pattern:** "In my role at [Company], I [what you did] which resulted in [outcome]. This directly maps to your need for [requirement from JD]."

### Paragraph 4: Why This Company (2-3 sentences)

Show you've researched the company. Reference something specific — a product, a recent announcement, a mission statement, a technical blog post. Explain why it resonates with YOUR goals.

**Anti-pattern:** "I admire your company's commitment to innovation." (Generic, could be sent to anyone.)

**Better:** "Your recent expansion into AI-assisted clinical decision support aligns with work I've done building NLP-to-FHIR conversion tools — I'd be excited to bring that hands-on AI-in-healthcare experience to your innovation pipeline."

### Paragraph 5: Close (2-3 sentences)

Brief, professional, confident. Restate your interest. Invite next steps. Thank them.

---

## Rules

1. **One page maximum.** If your cover letter is longer, cut it.
2. **No resume repetition.** Don't restate every bullet. Pick the 2-3 most compelling and frame them as a story.
3. **Ownership language throughout.** Same rules as the resume — no "assisted with," no "responsible for."
4. **Company-specific content.** At least one paragraph must be un-reusable — it should only work for this company. If you can send the same cover letter to two companies, it's too generic.
5. **Match the formality level.** A startup cover letter can be conversational. An enterprise/government letter should be more formal. Read the JD's tone for cues.
6. **Address gaps proactively.** If you're changing industries, explain the pivot. If you're missing a key skill, frame what you bring instead. Don't ignore the elephant.

---

## Cover Letter by Scenario

### Scenario: Standard Application (Strong Fit)
Lead with your strongest alignment. Focus body paragraphs on demonstrating direct experience. Keep it tight — you don't need to convince hard, just show the match clearly.

### Scenario: Career Pivot (Different Domain)
The hook must address the pivot directly. Frame it as intentional, not accidental. Show transferable skills with concrete examples. Explain why the new domain excites you with specificity.

### Scenario: Stretch Role (Under-Qualified on Paper)
Acknowledge the gap with confidence, not apology. Lead with what you DO bring that others don't. Show self-awareness and learning velocity. Use side projects or adjacent experience to cover gaps.

### Scenario: Internal Transfer
Reference institutional knowledge. Show how your current role's context gives you unique insight into the new role. Mention specific people, projects, or initiatives that connect the dots.

---

## Formatting

- **Font:** Match your resume's font (Aptos, Calibri, or Arial)
- **Size:** 11pt body, matching resume styling
- **Header:** Same contact block as your resume (creates visual consistency)
- **File name:** `Firstname_Lastname_CoverLetter.pdf`
- **Length:** 300-400 words ideal. Never exceed 500.
